Situation

Findependent, a financial advisory firm, was struggling to engage their target demographic of adults in their early to mid-20s. Despite offering valuable financial advisory services, the company lacked a digital presence that resonated with younger users who increasingly expect mobile-first experiences for managing their finances.

The firm recognized that traditional financial services often alienate younger users with complex terminology, intimidating interfaces, and products that don't address their specific life circumstances. Young adults in this demographic face unique financial challenges—from student loans and entry-level salaries to navigating financial independence for the first time—yet most financial apps are designed for established adults with more complex financial portfolios.

Findependent needed a mobile solution that could bridge this gap while positioning them as a trusted partner for financial planning rather than just another advisory service.

Task

Our challenge was to design a financial technology solution that would:

  • Financial Education: Demystify complex financial terminology and concepts to make finance accessible and less intimidating for young adults
  • Behavioral Change: Design features that would encourage positive financial behaviors, particularly around savings and investing habits
  • Value Proposition Enhancement: Improve Findependent's competitive positioning by creating unique value that would attract customers and drive revenue growth

Action

Our team executed a comprehensive user-centered design process:

User Research:

  • Conducted comprehensive research combining both in-person and virtual interviews with international students to understand their specific financial challenges and needs. This research helped validate our hypothesis that international students represented a significant opportunity for Findependent.

Persona Development & Journey Mapping:

  • Created detailed user personas based on research findings and mapped complete user journeys to identify key pain points and opportunities for intervention. This foundational work ensured all design decisions were grounded in real user needs rather than assumptions.

Strategic Refinement:

  • Based on testing feedback, made significant strategic decisions includin: Streamlined the overall design for better coherence and usability, Scaled down the feature set to focus on core user needs within realistic development constraints, Removed complex investment features in favor of more foundational financial tools, Eliminated the document assistant to focus on core financial management needs

Result

Solution Delivered:

The International Student Financial Platform successfully addressed the unique needs of international students by providing accessible, educational, and culturally-sensitive financial tools.

Key Outcomes:

  • User-Centered Design Solution: Created a mobile experience specifically tailored to international students' financial needs, addressing pain points that existing financial apps overlook. The solution balanced financial education with practical tools, making complex financial concepts accessible to users navigating the US financial system for the first time.
  • Strategic Focus Achievement: The final prototype successfully addressed all three original objectives—demystifying financial terminology through clear, educational interfaces; encouraging positive financial behaviors through simplified, actionable features; and establishing Findependent's unique value proposition in serving international students.
  • Iterative Design Success: Demonstrated strong UX methodology by using usability testing feedback to make strategic product decisions, showing the value of user-centered design in creating focused, viable solutions rather than feature-heavy products that don't serve core needs.
